Hikkaduwa Turtle hatchery

Tourists who want to see the unusual sight of newly hatched turtles making their way to the sea must visit the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, which is situated on Sri Lanka's southern coast. The sea turtle population in Sri Lanka has been significantly preserved because to the hatchery, which was established in 1986.

The Sri Lanka Wildlife Conservation Society, a nonprofit with the mission of preserving sea turtle species, operates the hatchery. Visitors may discover more about these amazing animals as well as the conservation measures implemented by the hatchery to keep them safe from predators and other threats.

The turtle hatchery procedure is the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery's major draw. In this area, guests may watch as eggs develop in the incubation chamber before being released into the water. Tens of thousands of turtle hatchlings are released into the ocean each year by the hatchery. The discharge occurs at night because that is when the hatchlings may travel to the ocean in safety.

Visitors can view the rehabilitation of damaged turtles in addition to the hatchery procedure. Turtles harmed by humans or by natural disasters are given medical treatment and attention at the hatchery. They are returned to their original habitat once they have recovered. A small museum that informs visitors about the various sea turtle species found in Sri Lanka and the hazards they encounter in their natural environments is also located in the hatchery. Visitors can also learn about the hatchery's conservation initiatives and how they can support the cause.

The Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a valuable educational resource that also gives the neighborhood jobs. People from the local communities are employed by the hatchery to assist with the hatchery process and to inform tourists about the hatchery's conservation initiatives. In conclusion, for anyone curious about sea turtle conservation efforts, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is a great spot to visit. It plays a significant role in preserving sea turtle species that are in danger of extinction and offers tourists an educational experience.
